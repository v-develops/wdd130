Content

Home page

In the Home page you can find a introduction about the site's content. It shows the basic info about stargazing, night sky watching, and all the other space related cool stuff that we can do. This page contain infomations about how can you watch os take pictures of the astronomic events, what kind of equipments you should buy or bring to these events, some basic principles about stargazing, personal experiences & recommendations about planet photography and much more!

There is no need to do a login or register an Email in order to use the site, its open for everyone that wants to learn a bit more about this fascinating hobby. There will be also some photos around the page (my Own photos and other people's too) with the objective to demonstrate what to expect to see, or photograph with diferents types of equipments.

At the top of the page you will find two different links, one will lead you to an special calendar that is going to help you keep track on the events, and the other will tell you what planets are visible at naked eye and how to locate them!

The main goal of this site is to help amateurs discover more about this hobby and what they need. But since this site also provides basic information about the astronomic events (day and time) in a very accesible way, it is also going to help experient people to easily gater information and prepare themselves! Its very simple but also very helpfull!

To conclude, I'm not an expert myself, so in case you want to share information or something that is going to be helpfull to everyone, you can contact me via the Email located in the bottom of the site.

Happy stargazing and clear skyes for you to enjoy!

Images for the Home page

Astronomic calendar

The second page is going to have a monthly calendar, showing all the important astronomic events that are going to happen in the currently month! Located at the top of the page, there will also be two images showing the most exciting events from the month. The purpose of these images are to help the user to be excited and start preparations to watch the most spetacular and important ones.

The information is going to be updated from month to month, so no need rush and start making hundreds of preparations for the whole year!
Its always good to remember that if you want to share any information about an upcoming astronomic event that is not on the calendar, you can send an Email to me! I'll happily update it.

And if you want to go back to the Home page you can simply click on the logo, located at the top of the page.

Images for the Astronomic calendar

Planet visibility

The last page is going to give you information about the most important celestial bodies you can see in the night: The solar system planets!

Some planets like Jupiter and Venus are pretty easy to find when you look at the sky, but the others are a little bit more difficult to locate, so this page will tell you what planets are currently visible in the night sky and at what position or constelations they are! To locate them you can also use free apps that use GPS to help you find them all.

The solar system model in the top of the page is merely illustrative, it does not represent the actual place of the planets and neither their real rotation/translation speed.

And just like the Astronomic calendar, you can get back to the Main page by clicking the top page logo.

(Each planet is going to have their respective symbol right above the status of visible or invisible)
